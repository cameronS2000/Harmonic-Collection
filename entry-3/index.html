Photos Taken on an iPhone

This photograph exhbits the colors, textures, and shapes of scaffolding as well as a distorted landscape behind a plastic tarp. My favorite photographs are often of the mundane, monotonous, and boring things that we pass in our everyday lives. This construction site stands between the camera and an expansive view of the Swiss Alps. This image asks whether the mountains add to the beauty of the construction site or the construction site subtracts from the beauty of the mountains.

Depicted in this photo is a steam covered shower door, backed by a window shining light through various bottles. What makes this photo interesting to me is the variability of depth. The viewer can allow their eyes to switch between the foreground and background exploring the different textures, colors, and contrasts.

This photograph represents my idea of an ideal portrait. The subject looks well lit, comfortable, and in a natural setting. The smart phone shines in its ability to create spontaneous photos like these; capturing true moments in contrast to an artificially produced photoshoot.
